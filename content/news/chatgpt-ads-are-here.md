The ads are in. Brands like Expedia, Qualcomm, and Best Buy are now appearing directly inside ChatGPT responses — sometimes triggering as early as your first prompt. OpenAI confirmed the rollout to Adweek, framing it as a natural evolution of their "freemium" model.

The timing is deliberate. OpenAI is burning cash at an extraordinary rate — training runs, inference costs, datacenter bills — and a $157 billion valuation needs revenue that matches the hype. Ads are the oldest monetisation trick in the book.

But let's be direct: **ads fundamentally change the incentive structure of an AI assistant**.

When an advertiser pays to appear alongside an answer about "best travel booking apps," the model's output is no longer neutral. Even if OpenAI insists the ads are clearly labelled, the pressure to keep advertisers happy creates distortions that compound over time. It's the same problem Google Search has been slowly dying from for a decade.

This is the exact moment when the argument for open-source AI stops being ideological and starts being practical. When your assistant is commercially neutral — running locally, no accounts, no ad targeting — the outputs aren't shaped by whoever paid the most to be included in the context window.

Tools like [OpenClaw](https://openclaw.ai) route queries through open models via OpenRouter, with no advertising relationship between the response and any brand. You can run Qwen, Llama, or Mistral and the answer is just... the answer.

The counterargument is that most users won't care. They didn't care about Google's ads either, and Google still has 90% of search market share. That might be true. But the users who *do* care — developers, researchers, anyone working with sensitive or commercial data — now have a concrete, practical reason to move elsewhere.

ChatGPT was always going to become a media property eventually. It's happening faster than most expected. The question is whether the open ecosystem is ready to absorb the users who leave when it does.

**The answer, increasingly, is yes.**
